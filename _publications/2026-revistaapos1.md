abstract: |
  This study analyzes urban sprawl and the process of slumification in Fortaleza, Ceará, investigating the intrinsic relationship between urban sprawl, public housing policies, and the persistence of socio-spatial inequalities. From a socio-historical perspective, the paper contextualizes the formation of peripheral communities in the country's fourth largest capital, highlighting the exponential growth of these settlements in recent decades and the consequent territorial segregation. The theoretical framework discusses the impacts of socioeconomic vulnerability on public safety, health, and, fundamentally, on the cognitive and educational development of individuals, which is frequently aggravated by the stress of urban violence. The findings indicate that historical state interventions operated in a palliative or exclusionary manner, subordinated to conflicts of economic interest. It concludes that overcoming the slumification scenario and guaranteeing the right to the city require unified, intersectoral urban planning, whose fundamental premise rests on the consolidation of a robust, comprehensive, and emancipatory basic education capable of breaking the generational cycle of social inequality.
