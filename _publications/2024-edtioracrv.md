abstract: |
  To discuss public and social policies within a context of scientific, social, and political influxes is to engage with
  categories that provoke meaningful reflections on our role as social actors who transform realities and re-signify knowledge.
  In this sense, the work Epistemological Trajectories in Public and Social Policies: Contemporary Scenarios and Flows presents a
  convergence of political and social phenomena that permeate the Brazilian context today. To this end, it categorizes and
  explains the scenarios that give rise to the problems faced in society within the field of public and social policies, while
  also highlighting the theoretical and practical nuances and trajectories that emerge from collective debate. Within this
  corpus, areas such as cultural policy, the environment, health, neo-developmentalism, social psychology, education, social
  work, human rights, adolescence, aging, gender issues, and inclusion are themes that shape the ideas discussed by the authors.
  These are approached from contemporary perspectives, offering indicative insights into the main political and social
  transformations occurring within society.
