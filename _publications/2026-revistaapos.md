abstract: |
  The field of governance has taken new directions in recent years with the emergence of new artificial intelligence
  technologies. Monitoring the use of these tools has become crucial for maintaining their security, ensuring optimal usability
  for users, and supporting the development driven by big tech companies. In the context of the Global South, this technology
  has once again relegated the countries comprising the bloc to a supporting role in its development, turning them into passive
  data providers for Northern countries to enhance the development of these technologies. To this end, investments in diverse
  sectors—such as education, healthcare, and precision agriculture solutions—as well as cooperation among Global South countries
  through alliances that promote thoughtful and regulated policies, may present themselves as viable alternatives for advancing
  the transition of power in the use of AI. The role of governance, thus, becomes indisputable in regulating, guiding, and
  enabling new advances in this field, supporting the success of countries in subverting the imperialist logic that has always
  dominated the realm of new technological discoveries. Furthermore, in addition to these applications, ethics in data handling
  and technology implementation must be promoted, fostering a more appropriate process of resource management.
